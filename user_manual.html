PayFlow HR & Payroll System

Complete User Manual · English · Documents app version v.2026.07.04-14:00
Live app: https://calexcl.github.io/MyPRSys/

1Introduction

PayFlow is MyEden Group's in-house HR and payroll application. It manages the complete employee lifecycle — from onboarding, through monthly payroll and payslip generation, to resignation and separation — for multiple companies within the group from a single screen.

What PayFlow does

  • Employee database — full profiles across 8 tabs: personal details, employment, salary history, statutory settings, leave ledgers, allowances, other income, and separation records.
  • Multi-company management — register each group entity (with its own logo, registration number, EPF and SOCSO employer numbers), switch between them, and assign employees to one or more companies.
  • Guided monthly payroll — a step-by-step wizard that handles overtime, allowances, new-joiner and resignation proration, unpaid leave deductions, and Malaysian statutory contributions (EPF/KWSP, SOCSO, EIS, PCB).
  • Payslips — professional PDF payslips per employee, downloadable individually or as a combined file, plus CSV/JSON exports of full payroll runs.
  • Reports — year-to-date totals, per-period summaries, and per-employee payroll history.
  • Backups — save your database to the browser, download it as JSON/CSV/Excel, or back it up to a shared Google Drive folder and restore it on any PC.
  • MYAL Calculator — a built-in tool for annual leave entitlement and resignation notice period calculations under the Employment Act 1955, synced with the employee database.

How the app is built (useful to know)

  • PayFlow is a single self-contained web page hosted on GitHub Pages. There is no server and no login — it runs entirely inside your browser.
  • All data is stored locally in the browser you use (see Section 12 for what this means and how to move data between PCs).
  • An internet connection is needed to open the page, generate PDF payslips (the PDF libraries load from a CDN), and use Google Drive backup. Everything else works once the page is loaded.

2Getting Started

1

Open the app

Go to https://calexcl.github.io/MyPRSys/ in a desktop browser. Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge on a PC is recommended — payroll tables and PDF generation are designed for a large screen.

2

Set up your companies first

Before adding employees, go to Companies in the sidebar and register each group entity (see Section 4). Employee IDs, payslip headers, and statutory employer numbers all come from the company record.

3

Add or import employees

Add employees one at a time with Add Employee, or bulk-import from a CSV/Excel file using the template provided on the Dashboard (see Section 5).

4

Run your first payroll

Once employees have salary records, open Run Payroll and follow the wizard (see Section 8).

5

Make a backup

After any significant data entry, click Save on the Dashboard and save a backup to Google Drive or download a JSON file. Get into this habit early — see the warning below.

⚠ Important — where your data lives. PayFlow stores everything in this browser on this computer. If you clear the browser's site data, use a different browser, use incognito/private mode, or switch to another PC, your data will not be there. Google Drive or JSON backups are the way to protect and move your database. See Section 12.
Seeing an old version after an update? Browsers sometimes cache the previous release. Press Ctrl + Shift + R (hard refresh) to force-load the latest version. The version stamp at the bottom of the app's sidebar tells you which release you are on.

The active company concept

PayFlow is multi-company. One company is always the active company (shown in the sidebar badge). The Employees list, payroll runs, and payslips you see are those belonging to the active company. An employee can be assigned to more than one company, with separate employment details per company (see Section 7).

Feedback and confirmations

  • Toasts — short messages in the corner confirm actions ("Company added", "Employee saved", etc.).
  • Confirmation dialogs — destructive actions (delete employee, clear backups, restore) always ask "Are you sure?" first.
  • Save status — the Dashboard shows whether your data is saved, when it was last saved, and where (Browser / Drive / file).

Only name is strictly required; sensible defaults fill the rest.

Employee table controls

Below the import panel, the Dashboard lists employees with a live search box, a department filter, a status filter, and a sort selector (the chosen sort mode is remembered between visits).

6Employees

The Employees screen lists everyone assigned to the active company, with columns for Employee ID, Name, Department, Job Title, Status, Date Confirmed, Type, Basic Salary, and Last Increment. Click any row to open the full Employee Profile.

7Employee Profile — the 8 Tabs

Opening an employee shows their profile with eight tabs. Click Save after editing; Back returns to the list.

Multi-company employees. An employee can be assigned to several companies. Company "pills" at the top of the profile let you pick which company's employment block you are viewing — the Employment, Separation, Salary tabs are per company, while Personal Info and Leave Management are shared across all companies.

7.1 · Personal Info

Name, Chinese name, NRIC/passport (with auto-DOB extraction), gender, date of birth, age, nationality, race, marital status, spouse name, address, contact number, email, emergency contacts (add as many as needed), and remarks.

7.2 · Employment

  • Company assignment (assign to / remove from companies), Employee ID (auto or manual), Department, Job Title, Employment Type.
  • Date Joined, Status (UP/CF/R/TM), Date Confirmed.
  • Working hours — From/To times and Meal Duration (minutes). These determine the actual working hours per day used in the overtime hourly-rate calculation.
  • OT Days divisor — the working-days figure (default 26) used to derive the daily rate: daily rate = basic ÷ OT days.

7.3 · Separation

Complete this tab only when an employee is leaving:

  • Resignation Letter Date — the day notice was tendered.
  • Notice period — auto-calculated from length of service using Employment Act s.12 defaults: under 2 years → 4 weeks; 2–5 years → 6 weeks; 5 years and above → 8 weeks (the in-app rule table is editable in the MYAL Calculator). The resulting Notice Period End Date and Resigned / Last Working Day are filled in automatically but remain manually editable.
Set the last working day! A resigned/terminated employee with no last-working-day on record is flagged in payroll and is not auto-included — their final salary cannot be prorated without a date.

7.4 · Salary & Statutory

  • Salary history — every salary is a dated record with a code, effective date, amount, and remark. Add a new record for each increment; the payroll wizard lets you pick which salary code applies. The Employees list shows the Last Increment automatically.
  • Hourly flag — a salary record can be marked hourly (for part-timers). Payroll then asks for hours worked and computes basic = rate × hours.
  • Statutory numbers — employee's KWSP No., SOCSO No., Income Tax (TIN) No.
  • Bank details — Bank Name and Account No., printed on the payslip.
  • Contribution switches — per-employee opt-outs for EPF, SOCSO, and EIS, separately for the employee share and the employer share. Leave all ON for a normal employee.
  • First-time contributor aged 57+ — an opt-in flag that exempts SOCSO/EIS for employees who first register after age 57, per PERKESO rules.

7.5 · Leave Management

Leave is shared across all companies — one balance per person.

  • Annual Leave entitlement — auto-set from service-length bands (Employment Act s.60E defaults): first 2 years → 8 days; 2–5 years → 12 days; over 5 years → 16 days. In the employee's first calendar year the entitlement is prorated from the join date. The auto value keeps itself up to date until you manually edit it, after which your figure is kept.
  • Brought Forward (BF) — days carried in from the previous year.
  • KPI bonus leave — extra AL granted by performance grade: Unqualified 0 · Average 1 · Satisfactory 3 · Excellent 6 · Manual Entry (any figure).
  • Sick leave — default 14 days per year; Replacement leave — granted via payroll (see 8).
  • Usage ledger — leave taken is recorded monthly (through the payroll wizard's Leave step) and accumulates into Used This Month / Used To Date / Balance figures, which are printed on the payslip.
  • On resignation — once the employee has exited all companies, the entitlement is automatically prorated to the last working day, and any leave taken above the prorated entitlement can be converted to an unpaid-leave deduction in the final payroll.

7.6 · Allowances

Recurring or one-time payments added on top of basic salary:

  • Each allowance has a type/name, amount, recurring Y/N, and optional start date and expiry date (an expired or not-yet-started recurring allowance is skipped automatically in payroll).
  • Statutory flags — mark whether the allowance is subject to KWSP, SOCSO, and EIS. These flags decide whether the amount is included in each contribution base.

7.7 · Other Income

The same structure as Allowances, for items such as commission or bonus, with its own KWSP-liability flag. Included in gross pay each payroll run.

7.8 · Data & Backup

The in-profile backup panel shows the backup history (timestamp, employee count, size, remark, location) with actions to view the raw JSON, download, restore, or clear all backups. Automatic snapshots are also created before risky operations such as imports and restores.

8Run Payroll — the Monthly Wizard

Open Run Payroll. The wizard walks through seven stages; you can move back and forth without losing entries. It processes the active company only.

1

Select Payroll Period

Choose the month/year (the last 12 months are offered). A banner previews how many employees will be included: all active employees, plus any resigned/terminated employees whose last working day falls inside this period ("resigned payable"). Resigned employees with no last-working-day date are listed but left unchecked, with a warning telling you to set the date in the Separation tab first.

2

Select Employees

Tick/untick who to include (a select-all control is provided). Each row shows the employee's latest salary code and amount.

3

Configure Salary, OT & Deductions

Per employee, set:

  • Salary code — which salary-history record to pay (defaults to the latest).
  • OT hours — overtime pay is computed as OT hours × hourly rate × 1.5, where the hourly rate is (basic ÷ OT days) ÷ actual daily working hours derived from the profile's working hours minus meal break.
  • Part-time hours — for hourly-flagged salaries, the hours worked this month (basic = rate × hours).
  • Extra allowance / extra deduction — one-off amounts for this run only.
  • Proration — auto-detected. If the employee joined during this period, a new-joiner adjustment is pre-computed on a calendar-day basis and pre-ticked; if they resigned during this period, a resignation adjustment (paying only up to the last working day) is pre-computed likewise. Both amounts are editable and can be unticked.
4

Leave Entries

Record this month's leave per employee: Replacement Leave Given, Annual Leave Taken, Compassionate, MC/Sick, Hospitalisation, Maternity, Paternity, and Unpaid Leave days. The wizard warns if an entry would push usage over the entitlement. Unpaid leave is deducted from gross on a calendar-day basis (basic ÷ calendar days in month × days). For final-month resignees, any annual leave taken beyond the prorated entitlement is auto-detected as excess AL and offered for conversion to an unpaid deduction.

5

Statutory Review

The wizard shows the calculated EPF, SOCSO, and EIS contributions (employee and employer shares) for every person, using the official bracket tables and the employee's age at period end (see Section 11). Every figure is editable as a per-run override if you need to match an external calculation. PCB (monthly tax) is auto-estimated but only deducted if you tick its checkbox — it is OFF by default; the estimated figure is shown for reference and can also be overridden.

6

Review Summary

A full payroll register: per-employee Basic, OT Pay, Allowance, Other Income, Unpaid Leave, Gross, employee-share EPF/SOCSO/EIS/PCB, extra deductions, Net Pay — plus the employer-share columns and grand totals. Columns that are zero for everyone are hidden automatically to keep the table readable.

7

Payroll Complete — approve & distribute

  • Approving saves the payslips into the database (they then appear in the Payslips and Reports sections, and the month's leave entries are posted to each employee's ledger).
  • Download payslips — one by one, all as individual PDFs, or as a single combined PDF. Filenames default to NAME_EMPID_PERIOD and are editable before download.
  • Export the run — full payroll data as CSV or JSON for records or bank submission preparation.
Payslip contents. Each PDF payslip carries the company letterhead (logo, registration no., address), employee details (ID, NRIC, department, job title), the full earnings and deductions breakdown, employer contributions, net pay, bank details, and the employee's leave summary table for the month.

9Payslips

The Payslips screen lists every approved payslip for the active company, newest period first.

  • Filter — the period dropdown narrows the list to one month, or shows all.
  • View / Download — open any payslip on screen or download its PDF (a progress bar shows PDF rendering status).
  • Export CSV / Export JSON — export every listed payslip's data in one file for archiving or further processing in Excel.
Payslips are tagged to the company that generated them — switch the active company to see another entity's payslips.

10Reports

Reports are built from approved payslips (run payroll at least once to see data):

  • Headline cards — Total Gross (YTD), Total Net Pay (YTD), Total EPF (employee + employer, YTD), Total PCB Tax (YTD).
  • Payroll by Period — gross, net, and payslip count per month.
  • Per-Employee Summary — cumulative gross, net, EPF, PCB, and months paid for every employee.

11Statutory Calculation Reference

This section explains exactly how PayFlow computes Malaysian statutory contributions, so you can verify any payslip figure.

Contribution bases

ContributionBase used
EPF / KWSP & EISBasic (after proration) + OT pay + KWSP-flagged allowances + KWSP-flagged other income
SOCSOBasic (after proration) + OT pay + SOCSO-flagged allowances

Rules applied

SchemeRule
EPF (KWSP)Official bracket table lookup. Under 60: employee 11% / employer 12% pattern (table values). Age 60+: employee 0% / employer 4%. Above the RM20,000 table ceiling, the exact percentage is used and rounded up to the nearest ringgit. Applies at all ages.
SOCSO (PERKESO)Wages capped at RM6,000. Under 60: standard table, both shares — from June 2026 the employee's share comprises two components, Invalidity plus the new Non-Employment Injury scheme (e.g. at the RM6,000 cap: employee RM74.40, employer RM104.15). Age 60+: employer-only Injury-scheme table (employee share forced to zero) — unchanged by the June 2026 revision. Applies from age 18. Exempt if the "first-time contributor aged 57+" flag is set.
EIS (SIP)Wages capped at RM6,000. From June 2026 the shares are no longer equal: the employee pays the Non-Employment Injury rate and the employer pays a higher rate (at the cap: employee RM44.65, employer RM74.40). Periods before June 2026 use the previous equal-share table (RM11.90 each at the cap). Applies ages 18–60 only; also exempt under the 57+ first-timer flag.
PCB (MTD)Simplified estimate: annual chargeable = (gross − employee EPF) × 12, run through the resident progressive rate scale (0% to RM5,000 … up to 25% above RM400,000), divided by 12. Deducted only when its checkbox is ticked in the Statutory step — off by default.

Age is always measured as at the last day of the payroll month, so an employee turning 60 mid-month is treated as 60 for that whole period.

Automatic rate-era switching. The June 2026 SOCSO/EIS revision is date-aware: when you run payroll for May 2026 or any earlier period, PayFlow automatically applies the pre-June-2026 tables; June 2026 onward uses the new tables with the Non-Employment Injury component. You do not need to select anything — the payroll period drives the choice. Already-approved payslips are stored values and are never recalculated.
Verify before filing. PayFlow's tables follow the published KWSP/PERKESO schedules and a simplified PCB scale, but statutory rates change from time to time and the PCB figure is an estimate, not a substitute for LHDN's official MTD computation. Always cross-check against the current official tables (or your statutory portal figures) before submission, and use the per-run override fields to align if needed.

12Data, Backups & Google Drive

How saving works

  • PayFlow auto-saves continuously to the browser's local storage — you rarely need to think about routine saving. The Dashboard indicator shows Saved / Unsaved / Error plus the last-saved time and location.
  • Local storage is per browser, per computer. It survives closing the tab and restarting the PC, but not clearing browsing data, and it does not follow you to another device.

The Save Backup dialog

Click Save on the Dashboard. Enter a remark (e.g. "Before July salary update"), then pick a location:

LocationWhat you get
BrowserA snapshot added to the in-app backup history (restorable from the Data & Backup panel).
Download — JSONA complete database file (payflow_backup_…​.json) saved to your computer. This is the full-restore format.
Download — CSV / ExcelThe employee list in spreadsheet form (employees only — not a full restore file).
Google DriveThe JSON backup uploaded to the shared MyEden Drive folder, retrievable from any PC.

Google Drive backup & restore

  • The first time you save to or load from Drive, a Google sign-in popup appears — sign in with the authorised company account and allow access. (If nothing appears, check that your browser isn't blocking popups.)
  • Load from Drive lists the JSON backups in the shared folder, newest first, with dates and sizes — pick one to restore.
  • The Drive folder is pre-configured inside the app, so all authorised users share the same backup location.

Restoring

  • From a file — Dashboard → Import JSON (or drag the file onto the drop zone).
  • From Drive — Dashboard → Load from Drive.
  • From history — Employee Profile → Data & Backup tab → Restore on any snapshot.
Restoring replaces the current database with the backup's contents. An automatic safety snapshot is taken first, but treat restores with care.
Moving to a new PC: on the old PC, Save → Google Drive (or download JSON). On the new PC, open the app → Load from Drive (or Import JSON). Done.

13MYAL Calculator

The MYAL Calculator (sidebar → Tools) computes annual leave entitlement and resignation notice periods under the Employment Act 1955. It has three tabs: Calculator, Employee Database, and Data Export.

13.1 · Calculator tab

  • Pick an employee from the searchable name dropdown (or enter dates manually). Click Sync Employees to pull the latest names and dates from the HR employee database.
  • Join Date + Resignation Letter Date — the notice period is auto-derived from length of service using the editable Notice Period Rules table (defaults per EA s.12: <2 years → 4 weeks · 2–5 years → 6 weeks · ≥5 years → 12 weeks). The Actual Notice End and Last Working Day can be nudged with the −/+ steppers or typed directly; a reset returns to the auto value.
  • No resignation? Use the Reference End Date instead, with Quick Set buttons — End of Year, Today, or a Custom Range.
  • AL Service Bands — the entitlement table (defaults per EA s.60E: first 2 yrs → 8 days · 2–5 yrs → 12 days · ≥5 yrs → 16 days) is fully editable: add, change, or remove bands.
  • Click Calculate Annual Leave Entitlement to see the total service period, the applicable band, and the (prorated, where applicable) leave entitlement.

13.2 · Employee Database tab

A lightweight roster synced from the HR system, with headline counts (Total / Active / Probation / Resigned), searchable table (name, join/confirm/resign dates, type, status, DOB, age, gender), plus Import Excel, Export Excel, Add Employee, and Clear All. Changes here auto-save to the browser.

13.3 · Data Export tab

  • Shows the MYAL storage details — record count, data size, and last-saved time. MYAL keeps its own cache under the key myal_calc_cache, separate from the main HR database at myal_employee_db.
  • Export — download the MYAL dataset as Excel or JSON; Import — load a JSON file.
  • Raw JSON view — inspect, copy, or paste-and-apply the raw data (advanced users only).
MYAL always syncs from the HR database first when it loads, so the calculator reflects the latest employee records automatically. Its own edits stay in its cache and do not overwrite HR data.
